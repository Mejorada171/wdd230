About Guadalajara

Guadalajara is the capital and largest city of the state of Jalisco in Mexico. It is the second-most populous metropolitan area in the country after Mexico City. Guadalajara is a vibrant and culturally rich city with a deep history, and it plays a significant role in Mexico's economy, culture, and society.

Population of Guadalajara

The total population of Guadalajara in 2020 was 1,385,629 inhabitants, with 51.9% being women and 48.1% men. The age groups that had the highest population were 20 to 24 years (117,797 inhabitants), 25 to 29 years (113,419 inhabitants), and 15 to 19 years (106,177 inhabitants).

Guadalajara is international

In June 2023, Guadalajara's international sales were $339 million, with a total of $619 million in international purchases. The main countries of origin for international purchases in 2022 were the United States ($1,999 million), China ($1,906 million), and Norway ($255 million).

Mexico and the steel

Mexico ranks 15th as a global producer of crude steel in 2021. Per capita consumption of finished steel products is 203 kg per year. The global average consumption was 233 kg per year. This has created 672,000 direct and indirect jobs and an investment of 14,461 million USD in the last 10 years (2012-2021).

Steel in Mexico 2022

The annual national production of finished products in 2022 is 19.7 million tons of steel.
